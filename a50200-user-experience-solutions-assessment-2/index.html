Assessment 2: create, test, refine, and present a UX solution.

This guide helps you complete Assessment 2 for A50200 User Experience Solutions. You continue from Assessment 1, use your existing research and persona, develop solution options, build a low-fidelity wireframe, test it with real users, refine it, and present the complete UX process.

Use the official wording first, then the explanation and evidence checklist. Your submission must show a clear line from research to ideation, solution, testing, refinement, and presentation.

Academic Integrity

AI Use Rules For This Assessment

Assessment Text

Permitted Use of AI: Students may use AI tools to clarify UX principles, understand wireframing and testing methods, brainstorm early solution ideas, organise written responses, improve clarity and grammar, generate rough layout ideas, suggest placeholder labels, structure presentation content, and practise communication.

Conditions of Use: All AI outputs must be reviewed, adapted, and refined by the student to match their specific solution. Students must demonstrate independent UX decision-making, explain and justify all design choices, and maintain consistency with original research and insights.

Not Acceptable Use of AI: AI must not generate final UX solutions, replace research-based decision making, fully create wireframes, create fake user feedback, simulate test participants, generate usability issues or improvements, or create full presentation scripts students cannot explain.

Explanation

AI can support your process, but the final design decisions, real user testing, feedback analysis, and refinements must be your own work. Treat AI as a study assistant, not as a substitute for your UX work.

Task 1

UX Solution Development And Validation

Task 1 is the full Assessment 2 project. It asks you to move from Assessment 1 research into solution creation, testing, refinement, and presentation.

Assessment Text

Assessment 2 - Create Solution Options and Validate Solution

In this assessment, you will continue developing the UX project from Assessment 1.

  • Generate solution ideas
  • Develop and communicate a UX solution
  • Create a wireframe prototype
  • Test your solution with users
  • Refine and present your final UX solution

Requirement: You must use the same project, research findings, and persona developed in Assessment 1.

Explanation

Think of Task 1 as one connected case study. Each question builds the next part of the UX story: problem, options, chosen solution, wireframe, testing, improvement, and presentation.

Evidence Checklist

Q1

Project Context And Problem Summary

Summarise the project you are continuing from Assessment 1.

Assessment Text

Provide a concise summary of your project. You must include:

  • Project name
  • The UX problem, refined based on research
  • Target user, based on your persona
  • Key insights from research, minimum 3

Your summary must be consistent with Assessment 1 and clearly show how research informed your understanding of the problem.

Explanation

Do not restart the project. Use the work already completed in Assessment 1 and summarise it clearly. Your problem should sound research-based, not like a personal opinion.

Suggested Structure

  • Project name: short and specific.
  • UX problem: one paragraph explaining the problem and who it affects.
  • Target user: name the persona or user type and key characteristics.
  • Research insights: list at least three insights and link each one to the problem.

Evidence Checklist

Q2

Solution Ideation And Opportunities

Create at least three solution ideas, then choose one to develop further.

Assessment Text

Based on your research findings, you must identify at least three solution ideas. For each solution idea, you must describe the idea clearly and explain which user problem it addresses and which user need it supports.

You must also identify potential benefits and potential limitations or risks. Present your ideas in a table with: solution idea, problem addressed, user need supported, benefits, and risks or limitations.

Final Selection: Select one solution to develop further. Justify your selection based on user impact, feasibility, and alignment with research findings.

Explanation

This question is about showing your thinking before you choose the final solution. Three ideas gives you options; the final selection shows your judgment.

IdeaWhat feature, flow, support, content, or interface change could solve the issue?
Problem addressedWhich exact user pain point from Assessment 1 does it target?
User needWhat does the user need to do, understand, feel, or complete?
BenefitsHow could the idea improve usability, clarity, confidence, speed, or satisfaction?
RisksWhat could be hard to build, confusing, costly, inaccessible, or unrealistic?
SelectionChoose the strongest idea using research alignment, impact, and feasibility.

Evidence Checklist

Q3

UX Solution Definition

Define the selected solution before wireframing it.

Assessment Text

You must clearly define your selected solution. Include a solution overview that explains what the solution is and what feature or part of the experience it improves.

Explain how your solution improves usability, desirability, and functionality. Describe how the user will interact with the solution and the key steps in the user flow.

Explain how you will test the solution and what you will evaluate, such as usability, clarity, and task success.

Explanation

This is where your selected idea becomes a clear UX solution. You are preparing the logic behind the wireframe: what it does, why it improves the experience, how users move through it, and how you will test it.

Answer In Four Parts

  • Solution overview: what the solution is and what part of the experience it improves.
  • UX principles: explain usability, desirability, and functionality separately.
  • User interaction: write the main steps the user follows.
  • Testing approach: explain what task users will complete and what you will measure or observe.

Evidence Checklist

Q4

Wireframe Development

Create at least one low-fidelity wireframe of the selected solution.

Assessment Text

You must create at least one low-fidelity wireframe of your solution. Your wireframe must represent your selected solution, clearly show layout and structure, include key elements such as navigation, buttons, and content areas, and be understandable without explanation.

You may hand draw and scan, or use digital tools such as Figma, Canva, or PowerPoint.

You must include: current state screenshot or brief description, proposed wireframe visual representation, and explanation of what has changed and why.

Explanation

A low-fidelity wireframe is not a finished graphic design. It is a clear layout showing structure, flow, and interaction. The assessor needs to see what changed from the current experience and why the change helps the user.

Current stateScreenshot or short description of what the user currently experiences.
Proposed wireframeVisual layout showing the selected solution.
NavigationShow menus, links, tabs, back buttons, or other movement controls.
ActionsShow key buttons, forms, inputs, filters, or call-to-action elements.
Content areasShow headings, labels, messages, cards, instructions, or information areas.
ExplanationDescribe what changed and why it improves the UX problem.

Evidence Checklist

Q5

User Testing And Feedback

Test the wireframe with at least 3 participants and refine the solution.

Assessment Text

You must test your solution with the same or similar users, minimum 3 participants. Present your wireframe to users and ask them to complete a task and provide feedback.

Document feedback in a table with: user, feedback, identified issue, and suggested improvement.

Analyse feedback by identifying at least 2 common issues and at least 2 improvement opportunities. For final refinement, explain what changes you will make and why these changes improve the UX.

Explanation

This question proves that your solution was checked with real people. The feedback table should record what participants actually said or did, then your analysis should identify patterns and improvements.

Testing Plan Starter

  • Participant: choose someone similar to your target user or persona.
  • Task: ask them to complete a realistic action using the wireframe.
  • Observation: note where they pause, misunderstand, succeed, or ask questions.
  • Feedback: record direct comments in simple language.
  • Refinement: choose changes based on repeated problems, not only one random comment.

Evidence Checklist

Q6

Final UX Solution Presentation

Record a professional 5-10 minute video presentation.

Assessment Text

Prepare and deliver a 5-10 minute video presentation explaining your project from start to finish. Your presentation must clearly show your UX process, how your solution was developed based on research, and how your design improves the user experience.

Your presentation must include: project overview, target user, UX problem, minimum 3 research insights, selected solution and justification, wireframe and explanation, testing results and improvements, final solution, and expected impact.

Your presentation must use a presentation tool such as PowerPoint or Canva, include visual elements such as wireframes or diagrams, clearly explain your decisions, and follow the UX process: problem to research to insights to solution to testing to refinement.

You must record yourself presenting with video and audio, present to an audience, and include at least 2 audience questions about your project.

Explanation

Your video is the final story of your UX process. It should not sound like reading answers from the template. Use visuals and explain what you did, why you did it, what you learned, and how the final solution improved.

Slide 1Project overview, social issue or context, and target user.
Slide 2UX problem and 3 research insights.
Slide 3Selected solution and justification linked to research and user needs.
Slide 4Wireframe and key interaction steps.
Slide 5Testing method, participant feedback, and improvements.
Slide 6Final solution, expected impact, and 2 audience questions.
