Assessment 1: Plan to promote yourself to potential clients.

This guide helps you complete Assessment 1 for A50207 Early Adopters Freelancing. It keeps the original assessment wording visible, explains each requirement in simpler language, and shows the evidence students need to prepare.

In this assessment you build your early freelance UX identity. You identify industry contacts, participate in professional networks, prepare a business plan, then apply that plan in a simulated eHarmony client project with a proposal, contract, invoice, and payment follow-up process.

Student Support

Freelance UX Service Options

Use these examples to choose services that match your current ability. Do not claim expert services you cannot explain or deliver.

UX audit

Review an app or website, identify usability problems, and recommend improvements.

User research support

Create surveys, interview scripts, consent forms, and research summaries.

Wireframes

Design low-fidelity screens that show layout, hierarchy, and user flow.

Prototype design

Create clickable screens in Figma, Lovable, Replit, Bolt, or another tool.

Landing page redesign

Improve content structure, calls to action, trust signals, and visual hierarchy.

Usability testing

Plan tasks, observe users, collect feedback, and document findings.

Accessibility review

Check contrast, readability, headings, keyboard flow, and form labels.

Design documentation

Prepare handover notes, design rationale, annotations, and client-ready files.

A beginner freelance plan can still be professional. The key is honesty: define a clear scope, realistic pricing, realistic timelines, and evidence that you understand the client relationship.

Academic Integrity

AI Use Rules For This Assessment

Assessment Text

Acceptable Use of AI - Assessment 1 (Plan to Promote Self to Potential Clients)

AI tools may be used to support planning and structuring. However, you must demonstrate your own networking activities, business planning, and client engagement decisions. AI must not generate your evidence, strategy, or business outputs.

Permitted Use of AI: Clarifying freelance, UX business, and networking concepts; brainstorming ideas for contacts, services, and strategies; structuring responses; improving clarity, grammar, and formatting.

Not Acceptable Use of AI: Generating full business plans, contracts, or proposals; creating fake contacts, networking evidence, or participation; generating pricing, negotiation, or financial decisions without reasoning; submitting content that is not specific, realistic, or cannot be explained.

Explanation

AI can help you understand and organise, but your evidence must come from real actions you take. Do not invent contacts, screenshots, specialist advice, signatures, invoices, or payment events.

Establishing Your UX Network

Task 1 asks you to show that you are beginning to connect with the UX industry, not just writing about networking in theory.

Assessment Text

Scenario

You are at the early stage of positioning yourself as a freelance UX designer. To build a sustainable career, you need to actively develop and maintain professional relationships within the UX industry and demonstrate participation in relevant professional networks. You are expected to not only identify contacts but also demonstrate how these relationships contribute to your professional reputation and ongoing engagement with industry practices.

Explanation

Think of this task as the beginning of your professional UX address book. You are showing who you could learn from, follow, contact, meet, or collaborate with.

Evidence Checklist

T1 Q1

Identify and Maintain Industry Contacts

Students need five relevant contacts and a realistic follow-up plan for each one.

Assessment Text

What you must do

You must identify key industry contacts that are relevant to your future career as a UX designer. These contacts should support your professional growth, networking opportunities, and potential employment or freelance work. You should demonstrate that these contacts are realistic and could be maintained over time.

Answer requirements

  • Provide a minimum of five (5) industry contacts
  • For each contact, include: Name; Contact details (e.g. website, email, LinkedIn, or phone); A clear explanation of why this contact is relevant to your career; A brief explanation of how you would maintain or follow up this contact over time (e.g. networking events, LinkedIn engagement, collaboration)
  • Contacts must reflect current UX/design industry relevance
Explanation

A contact does not need to be your close friend. It can be a UX designer, product designer, design recruiter, UX lecturer, design studio, industry mentor, meetup organiser, or professional community leader.

Contact type Why it can be relevant Possible follow-up
UX designer or product designer Can show industry practice, portfolio examples, tools, and career pathways. Connect on LinkedIn, comment on posts, ask one thoughtful question, attend their event.
Design studio or agency Potential freelance or internship pathway. Follow company updates, review portfolio work, send a short introduction.
Recruiter or hiring manager Can help you understand UX job expectations and portfolio requirements. Ask about junior UX requirements and keep your profile updated.
Mentor, trainer, or lecturer Can review your work and guide professional development. Request feedback, share your progress, ask for advice on portfolio evidence.

Sentence starters

Why relevant: "This contact is relevant because they work in / support / recruit for / teach / organise..."

Maintain contact: "I would maintain this contact by following their professional updates, attending events, and sending a short follow-up message after relevant discussions."

Task 2

Design a Professional Business Plan

Task 2 asks you to build a realistic freelance business plan based on your current skills, goals, services, finances, and legal responsibilities.

Assessment Text

Scenario

Building on your network from Task 1, you are now preparing to formally promote your services as a freelance UX designer. You will develop a structured business plan that outlines how you will operate, manage finances, attract clients, and sustain your professional reputation.

You are expected to demonstrate practical and realistic approaches to managing freelance work, including financial systems, documentation processes, and compliance with relevant business requirements.

Explanation

This task is like your freelance operating manual. It should answer: What services do I offer? Who will pay for them? How much? How will I schedule work? How will I manage money, tax, contracts, risks, and learning?

Evidence Checklist

T2 Q1

Develop a Strategic Business Plan

This is the largest single response in the assessment. Use the required headings exactly so the assessor can find every part.

Assessment Text

What you must do

You must develop a detailed business plan that outlines how you will promote your UX services, manage your work, and maintain a sustainable freelance business. Your business plan must address all of the following sections. Your responses must demonstrate realistic, applied planning based on actual industry engagement, not hypothetical or generic answers.

Your business plan must address all of the following sections:

  1. Strategy Overview: Clearly describe your strategy for promoting yourself as a UX designer. Include specific actions and channels you will use. Demonstrate how your strategy is informed by real industry practices or platforms.
  2. Goals and Expected Results: Define measurable goals for your UX business. Provide specific and measurable goals, describe expected outcomes, and explain how success will be evaluated using clear metrics.
  3. Services and Pricing: Describe the UX services you will offer. Provide a realistic and structured fee model, including service types, pricing, and payment methods. Briefly justify how your pricing supports viable cash flow.
  4. Work Schedule Plan: Outline your availability and work schedule. Provide a realistic 12-month work plan and demonstrate how you will manage workload sustainably.
  5. Financial Management Strategy: Provide estimated annual income, estimated annual expenses, a sample invoice template, and systems or tools for invoicing, financial records, and expense tracking.
  6. Regulatory, Taxation, and Insurance Requirements: Identify relevant regulations, taxation obligations, insurance requirements, and reliable sources of information such as ATO, business.gov.au, accountant, or financial advisor.
  7. Service Contract: Provide a sample contract for your services with parties involved, terms and conditions, clear payment terms, responsibilities, and a variation clause.
  8. Negotiation Strategy: Explain how you will promote your services and describe negotiation techniques for pricing and timeframes. Include at least one practical example scenario.
  9. Professional Development: Identify at least 2 professional development needs and at least 2 opportunities to enhance your professional profile.
  10. Risk Management: Identify at least 2 potential risks and explain how you would manage or mitigate them.
  11. Personal and Professional Alignment: Explain how your business plan aligns with your quality of life goals, artistic goals, and commercial goals. Describe strategies to maintain balance.
Explanation

Write this as a structured document with the 11 headings. Every heading needs realistic details. Avoid vague phrases like "I will use social media" without explaining which platform, what you will post, how often, and how you will measure success.

Heading What students should include Helpful example direction
Strategy Overview Promotion channels, actions, portfolio plan, networking plan. LinkedIn profile, case studies, Behance, UX meetups, direct outreach.
Goals and Expected Results Measurable goals with dates and metrics. Clients, income, portfolio projects, connections, proposals sent.
Services and Pricing Service list, hourly or project pricing, payment methods, pricing justification. UX audit, wireframes, prototype, testing, landing page redesign.
Schedule and Finance 12-month plan, weekly hours, income, expenses, invoice system, record keeping. Google Sheets, Xero, MYOB, Excel, monthly folders, invoice numbering.
Legal and Risk ABN, GST awareness, tax, insurance, contract clauses, scope creep, non-payment. Use ATO and business.gov.au as sources. Mention accountant advice if relevant.

Task 3

Simulated Freelance Engagement

Task 3 applies your business plan to a client scenario. You are an emerging freelance UX designer working on an eHarmony landing page redesign for the Australian market.

Assessment Text

Scenario - Simulated Freelance UX Engagement

You are working as an emerging freelance UX designer based in Australia. As part of building your professional practice, you have been approached by eHarmony, an established international online dating platform, to undertake a UX design project.

The client has requested a redesign of the landing page for the Australian audience. The redesigned landing page must clearly communicate platform safety and trustworthiness, privacy protection and responsible data use, and the quality and credibility of the service.

This project requires you to balance business objectives, user needs and expectations, and ethical considerations in UX design such as transparency, informed consent, and avoiding misleading design.

Explanation

Even though the client is simulated, your documents should look professional. Imagine you are sending them to a real client who needs to understand scope, pricing, timeline, responsibilities, changes, payment, and trust.

T3 Q1

Develop a Business Proposal

Create a client-ready proposal document, not just short notes.

Assessment Text

What you must do

You must prepare a professional business proposal for your client based on your business plan developed in Task 2. This proposal must reflect a realistic freelance engagement and demonstrate how you would clearly communicate project scope, costs, and expectations to a client.

Your proposal must be written in a professional format suitable for a real client (e.g. document, slide deck, or formatted PDF).

Answer requirements: Include project outcomes; fees with structure and justification; project schedule with milestones; additional expenses; client communication and agreement.

Evidence requirements: A complete business proposal document and evidence of client communication or agreement, such as an email draft, screenshot, written confirmation, or simulated client response.

Explanation

Your proposal tells the client what you will deliver, how much it costs, when it will happen, what is included, and how agreement is confirmed. Make it polished enough to send as a PDF or professional document.

Proposal structure students can follow

1. Project summaryBriefly describe the eHarmony landing page redesign for Australian users.
2. OutcomesWireframes, high-fidelity mock-up, content structure, trust/privacy messaging, accessibility notes.
3. FeesTotal project price, breakdown, deposit, final payment, and pricing justification.
4. TimelineStart date, milestones, feedback dates, revision period, completion date.
5. Extra expensesStock images, testing tools, subscriptions, or state that no extra expenses are expected.

T3 Q2

Draft a Contract

Prepare a formal written contract for the simulated UX project.

Assessment Text

What you must do

You must develop a formal, written contract for this UX project that would be suitable for use in a real freelance engagement in Australia.

Under Australian contract law, a valid contract must include key elements such as offer and acceptance, consideration (exchange of value), intention to create legal relations, clear and certain terms, and capacity of all parties to enter into the agreement.

Answer requirements: Include parties to the contract; scope of work; consideration; payment terms; obligations of all parties; project timeline; variation and change management clause; communication and approval process; intellectual property and ownership; confidentiality and privacy; termination clause; dispute resolution; legal validity statements; signatures.

Evidence requirements: A complete, formatted contract document written in professional business language with realistic freelance practice, clear structure, legally relevant clauses, and usable real-world format.

Explanation

A contract protects both freelancer and client. It should clearly say who is involved, what work is included, what is not included, how payment works, how changes are handled, and what happens if there is a problem.

Clause Plain-English meaning
Scope of workExactly what you will deliver, such as wireframes, mock-up, revisions, and delivery format.
ConsiderationThe exchange of value: your UX services in return for client payment.
Variation clauseHow extra work, changed scope, extra cost, or changed deadlines will be agreed.
Intellectual propertyWho owns the final work and when ownership transfers.
ConfidentialityHow client information and sensitive data will be protected.

T3 Q3

Seek Specialist Advice

Get real feedback on the drafted contract and show what changed.

Assessment Text

What you must do

You must obtain a review of your drafted contract from a qualified specialist to ensure that your agreement reflects professional and legally appropriate standards.

Who can be considered a qualified specialist: A legal professional; a business advisor or mentor; a lecturer or trainer with experience in contracts or business; an industry professional with freelance or contract experience.

Answer requirements: Obtain feedback by sharing your contract and requesting feedback on clarity of terms, payment terms, and risks. Provide evidence such as email, message exchange, screenshot, meeting notes, or feedback document. Apply feedback by identifying at least 2 changes made to your contract and explaining what changed and why.

Explanation

This is not asking you to become a lawyer. It asks you to show professional behaviour by asking someone suitable to review your contract and then improving it based on feedback.

Message starter for requesting feedback

"Hi [Name], I am completing a simulated freelance UX contract for an assessment. Could you please review whether the payment terms, scope, variation clause, and responsibilities are clear? I would appreciate any feedback on risks or unclear wording."

T3 Q5

Respond to Client Changes

Handle a scope change professionally and ethically.

Assessment Text

Scenario

One week into the project, after you have completed initial wireframes for the eHarmony landing page, the client contacts you with significant changes to the project scope.

The client now requests a new section highlighting "success stories" with real user testimonials, integration of a video feature on the landing page to improve engagement, and additional emphasis on premium subscription benefits to increase conversions. They also request that these changes be delivered within the original timeline and no increase in the agreed project cost.

What you must do

Respond to the client's request in a professional and ethical manner using your contract and business practices as reference for your response.

Answer requirements: Adapt to the new requirements; maintain professional ethics and reputation; negotiate changes using your contract; provide a written response to the client that acknowledges the request, explains the impact, and proposes a revised solution.

Explanation

This is a classic scope creep situation. A professional freelancer does not simply say yes to extra work for free. You should be respectful, transparent, refer to the variation clause, and offer options such as revised cost, revised timeline, or reduced scope.

Professional response pattern

Acknowledge: "Thank you for outlining the additional landing page requirements."

Explain impact: "These additions affect scope because testimonials, video integration, and subscription messaging require extra design, review, and implementation planning."

Offer options: "Option A: keep the original timeline by moving some items to a later phase. Option B: include all additions with revised timeline and fee. Option C: prioritise only the highest-value addition within the original scope."

T3 Q6

Issue an Invoice

Create a professional invoice that matches your contract terms.

Assessment Text

What you must do

Prepare and issue a professional invoice to your client based on the agreed contract terms. The invoice must reflect a real freelance transaction and meet standard Australian invoicing requirements.

Answer requirements: Align with contract terms; include required invoice components; demonstrate accuracy and professionalism; align with your financial system from Task 2.

Your invoice must include: Title "Invoice" or "Tax Invoice" if GST applies; your name/business name; contact details; ABN if applicable; invoice number; invoice date; client details; description of services; itemised costs; total amount payable; payment terms and due date; payment method/details.

Evidence requirements: Submit a complete invoice document that is realistic, professionally formatted, and consistent with your contract.

Explanation

The invoice should look like a real document a client could pay from. Make sure the amount, payment stage, and due date match the contract. If you use "Tax Invoice", only do this if GST applies to your simulated business context.

Invoice item What to check
Invoice numberUse a unique number such as INV-001 or EH-UX-001.
DatesInclude invoice date and due date based on contract payment terms.
Client detailsUse eHarmony or simulated representative details.
Itemised costsShow project fee, milestone payment, hours, rate, or deposit clearly.
Payment methodInclude bank transfer or other payment details in a professional way.

T3 Q7

Manage Non-Payment

Show a professional follow-up process when payment is overdue.

Assessment Text

What you must do

You issued your final invoice to eHarmony with a payment term, as outlined in your contract. The due date has now passed by 10 days, and no payment has been received. The client has not responded to your initial invoice email. This payment is important for your cash flow, as you rely on it to cover your business expenses.

Explain how you would manage this situation professionally using your contract and business processes.

Answer requirements: Describe your follow-up process with progressive escalation; demonstrate professional communication; explain escalation steps; reference your contract; provide a clear timeline showing when you would act.

Explanation

Do not jump straight to legal threats. Start with polite reminders, then progressively escalate using the contract. Keep records of every email, date, and client response.

Timing Action Tone
10 days overdueSend polite reminder with invoice attached and due date noted.Friendly and professional.
14 days overdueSend firmer follow-up and request a payment date.Clear and respectful.
21 days overdueRefer to contract late payment terms and pause further work if relevant.Firm and documented.
30 days overdueEscalate according to contract, such as formal letter, mediation, advisor, or debt recovery process.Professional and evidence-based.
