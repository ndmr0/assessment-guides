Assessment 1: Define a design challenge and develop an innovative UX concept.

This guide helps you complete Assessment 1 for A50201 Ideation, Innovation and Design. It explains each task in plain English, shows what evidence is expected, and gives beginner-friendly options without writing the final answers for you.

In this assessment you first answer knowledge questions using the Airbnb booking case study. Then you define your own UX project idea, consider legal and industry requirements, create low-fidelity wireframes, and evaluate your design using functionality, ergonomics, aesthetics, and sustainability.

Student Support

Plain-English Word Bank

Target market The group of people your design is made for.
Medium The format or platform, such as mobile app, website, kiosk, or service system.
Technical requirements The features, tools, data, integrations, and systems needed to make the concept work.
Conceptualisation The process of turning research and ideas into a clear design concept.
Low-fidelity wireframe A rough sketch that shows layout, content, and flow before visual design.
Quality assurance Checks used to make sure the solution works properly and meets expectations.
Ergonomics How comfortable, efficient, readable, and easy the design is to use.
Sustainability How well the design can last, scale, adapt, and avoid unnecessary waste or effort.
Intellectual property Ownership and correct use of logos, text, images, designs, names, and original ideas.

AI Rules

Use AI As Support, Not As Your Final Answer

Permitted

  • Clarify concepts, research methods, design principles, and assessment wording.
  • Brainstorm early directions that you later refine yourself.
  • Improve grammar, clarity, and structure after you write your own draft.
  • Ask for feedback on whether your question or paragraph is clear.

Not permitted

  • Submitting full AI-generated answers as your own work.
  • Using AI to create your final design concept, wireframes, sketches, visuals, or evaluation.
  • Submitting generic content that does not connect to your case study or project.
  • Using AI instead of your own ideation, design decisions, and justification.

Simple rule: AI can help you understand and organise. You must make the design decisions and write the final assessment in your own words.

Task 1

Knowledge Questions - Airbnb Booking Case Study

Assessment Text

Case Study

You have been hired as a junior UX designer by Airbnb to support improvements to their booking experience.

Recent internal research has identified the following issues:

  • Many users begin a booking but do not complete it
  • Users report confusion around pricing (fees, taxes, total cost)
  • Cancellation policies are unclear and difficult to understand
  • First-time users feel overwhelmed by too many options and filters
  • Some users leave the platform to compare prices on competitors

The UX team has been asked to:

  • Analyse user behaviour and market trends
  • Generate design ideas to improve conversion rates
  • Ensure the final solution meets quality and business expectations

You will use this case study to answer the following questions.

Explanation

Task 1 is not about your own project yet. Every answer must clearly refer to the Airbnb booking experience and the problems listed in the case study.

Evidence Checklist - Task 1 Case Study

T1 Q1

Research Methodologies For Market Analysis

Explain two research methods that could help Airbnb understand users and the market.

Assessment Text

Q1. Research methodologies for market analysis

Based on the case study, explain at least two (2) research methodologies that could be used to analyse the market and user behaviour.

You must, for each methodology:

  • Name and clearly explain the methodology
  • Explain how it would be applied in this scenario
  • Describe what type of insights it would generate
  • Explain how these insights would support UX design decisions

Answer requirements:

  • You must write two (2) paragraphs (one per methodology)
  • Each paragraph must be at least 100 words
  • Each paragraph must contain 5-7 full sentences
  • Your answer must clearly reference the case study
Explanation

Choose two methods only, then explain each one deeply. A strong paragraph moves from definition, to Airbnb use, to insight, to design decision.

Method Options

Behaviour analytics Use booking funnel data to see where users abandon the booking process.
Competitor analysis Compare Airbnb pricing display and filters with Booking.com, Expedia, or hotel websites.
User surveys Ask users what confused them about price, cancellation, filtering, or trust.
Usability testing Observe users trying to complete a booking and identify specific moments of friction.

Safe Paragraph Structure

Sentence 1: name and define the method. Sentence 2: connect it to Airbnb. Sentence 3: explain what data it collects. Sentence 4: explain what insight it creates. Sentence 5: explain how that insight supports a UX decision. Sentence 6 or 7: connect back to conversion, clarity, or user confidence.

Evidence Checklist - T1 Q1

T1 Q2

Conceptualisation Process In UX Design

Explain how research becomes ideas, then wireframes, prototypes, and final design outputs.

Assessment Text

Q2. Conceptualisation process in UX design

Based on the case study, explain the conceptualisation process in UX design and how this process connects to the development and production of a design solution.

You must:

  • Describe the key stages involved in conceptualisation
  • Explain how research findings influence idea generation
  • Provide one (1) example of a design concept for this scenario
  • Explain how your concept addresses the UX problem
  • Explain how conceptual ideas are translated into design outputs (e.g. wireframes, prototypes, final product)

Answer requirements:

  • You must write one (1) paragraph
  • Your paragraph must be at least 100 words
  • Your paragraph must contain 5-7 full sentences
  • Your answer must clearly reference the case study
Explanation

This question asks for a process. Do not only describe one idea. Show the path from research, to concept, to wireframe, to prototype, to final product.

Research Understand booking drop-off, pricing confusion, policy clarity, and filter overload.
Ideate Generate possible fixes such as clearer price breakdowns or simpler filters.
Represent Turn the idea into sketches, wireframes, user flows, or clickable prototypes.
Produce Refine the design with feedback and prepare it for development.

Concept Examples You Could Discuss

  • A clearer total price panel that shows fees and taxes earlier in the booking flow.
  • A simplified first-time user booking mode with fewer filters and clearer recommendations.
  • A plain-English cancellation summary beside the booking confirmation button.

Evidence Checklist - T1 Q2

T1 Q3

Quality Assurance Processes In UX Design

Explain two QA processes that would help Airbnb check that the UX solution works as expected.

Assessment Text

Q3. Quality assurance processes in UX design

Based on the case study, discuss the importance of at least two (2) quality assurance processes that could be used to ensure the UX solution produces the expected outcomes.

You must, for each process:

  • Name and explain the process
  • Explain how it would be applied in this project
  • Describe what risks it helps prevent
  • Explain how it contributes to a successful UX solution

Answer requirements:

  • You must write two (2) paragraphs (one per process)
  • Each paragraph must be at least 100 words
  • Each paragraph must contain 5-7 full sentences
  • Your answer must clearly reference the case study
Explanation

Quality assurance means checking the quality before and after release. Your answer should explain what risk each process reduces for Airbnb users and the business.

QA Process Options

Usability testing Checks whether users can complete a booking, understand pricing, and find policy information.
Accessibility review Checks readability, keyboard access, colour contrast, labels, and inclusive use.
A/B testing Compares two design versions to see which improves booking completion or clarity.
Design review checklist Checks consistency, brand alignment, platform standards, copy clarity, and error states.

Task 2

Your Own UX Project

Assessment Text

Task 2 - Your own UX project

In this task, you will define and develop your own UX project idea. This project will continue throughout the subject and should represent a real-world UX opportunity.

You are encouraged to approach this as a product designer, identifying a meaningful problem and designing a solution that could realistically exist in the market.

Your project must focus on a social issue and should be something you are interested in exploring further.

You may choose:

  • An existing app, website, or system to improve, OR
  • A completely new UX idea that solves a real problem
Explanation

Choose one project and keep it consistent across Q1, Q2, Q3, and Q4. Your concept, legal requirements, wireframes, and evaluation should all describe the same solution.

Evidence Checklist - Task 2 Project Setup

T2 Q1

Project Concept Definition

Define your own UX concept across A-F: concept, purpose, medium, technical requirements, cost, and timeframe.

Assessment Text

Q1. Project Concept Definition

Based on your project brief and user persona, explain your design concept by addressing the following questions A-F:

  • You must provide a clear and structured response for each section (A-F)
  • Each section should be at least 100 words
  • Each section must contain 5-7 full sentences
  • Your responses must be consistent with your project idea

A. What is your main design concept? Clearly describe the core idea of your project.

B. What is the purpose of your design? Explain what your solution is trying to achieve.

C. What medium will you use to accomplish this purpose? Explain how your solution will be delivered.

D. What are the technical requirements? Describe what is needed to build your solution.

E. What are the estimated costs? Provide a basic estimate of what it would cost to develop your solution.

F. What is the estimated timeframe? Explain how long it would take to develop your solution.

Explanation

This is the foundation of your project. Treat A-F like a mini product brief. Each answer should be specific, realistic, and connected to the same user problem.

A. Concept

What are you designing? What problem does it solve? What makes it valuable or different?

B. Purpose

What outcome should improve for users? What will be easier, clearer, safer, faster, or more accessible?

C. Medium

Will it be a mobile app, website, kiosk, tablet tool, chatbot, dashboard, or service system? Why?

D. Requirements

List realistic features, data needs, tools, APIs, accessibility needs, and platform requirements.

E. Costs

Think about research, UX design, UI design, development, testing, hosting, maintenance, and content.

F. Timeframe

Break the work into stages such as research, ideation, wireframes, prototype, testing, build, launch.

Beginner Tip

Use rough but logical estimates. The assessor wants realistic thinking, not exact professional quotes.

Consistency Tip

If your concept is a mobile app in A, do not suddenly describe a website or unrelated system in C-F.

Useful Sentence Starters

The main design concept is a... / The purpose of this design is to... / This medium is appropriate because users... / The technical requirements include... / The main cost areas would be... / A realistic timeframe would be...

Evidence Checklist - T2 Q1

T2 Q2

Industry, Legal, And Compliance Considerations

Explain standards, intellectual property, and legal requirements for your project.

Assessment Text

Q2. Industry, Legal, and Compliance Considerations

For your project, answer the following A-C:

  • You must provide a response for each section (A-C)
  • Each section should be at least 100 words
  • Each section must contain 5-7 full sentences
  • Your answers must relate to your specific project

A. What industry standards will you consider? Explain any standards or best practices relevant to your design.

B. Are there any intellectual property (IP) requirements? Explain how IP applies to your project.

C. What legal requirements must be met? Explain any legal considerations.

Explanation

This question checks whether your idea is responsible and realistic. You do not need to be a lawyer. You need to show awareness of standards, ownership, privacy, accessibility, and project-specific risks.

A

Industry Standards

Consider usability, accessibility, responsive design, platform guidelines, plain language, design consistency, and secure interaction patterns.

B

IP Requirements

Consider original logos, images, icons, copy, interface patterns, brand names, student-created sketches, and avoiding direct copying.

C

Legal Requirements

Consider privacy, data collection, consent, accessibility, child safety, health information, financial information, or location data if relevant.

Evidence Checklist - T2 Q2

T2 Q3

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Create hand-drawn sketches that show multiple ideas and your design thinking process.

Assessment Text

Q3. Low-Fidelity Wireframes (Creative Process)

You are required to develop hand-drawn low-fidelity wireframes for your UX project.

This task focuses on your thinking process, not visual perfection.

You must:

  • Clearly show what you are designing
  • Include multiple ideas (not just one version)
  • Show your progression from initial ideas to final concept
  • Use annotations (arrows, notes, labels) to explain your thinking

You are encouraged to:

  • Explore different layouts and flows
  • Try different approaches before selecting one
  • Think about how the user moves through your design

Submission requirements:

  • Scan or photograph all your sketches
  • Include early drafts, iterations, and final concept
Explanation

The assessor wants to see your thinking. Rough sketches are acceptable, but they must be readable, labelled, and connected to your project concept.

Draft 1: layout idea Draft 2: alternate flow Draft 3: improved navigation Final concept with notes

What Your Wireframe Evidence Should Show

  • At least two or three different layout or flow attempts.
  • Arrows showing movement from screen to screen or step to step.
  • Labels such as search, filter, profile, confirmation, help, submit, or next.
  • Notes explaining why you changed something between versions.
  • A final concept that is clearer than the early drafts.

Evidence Checklist - T2 Q3

T2 Q4

Design Evaluation

Evaluate your wireframes using functionality, ergonomics, aesthetics, and sustainability.

Assessment Text

Q4. Design Evaluation

After completing your wireframes, explain your design by addressing the following A-D:

  • You must provide a response for each section (A-D)
  • Each section should be at least 100 words
  • Each section must contain 5-7 full sentences
  • Your answers must clearly relate to your wireframes

A. Functionality: Explain how your design works.

B. Ergonomics: Explain how your design supports user comfort and ease of use.

C. Aesthetics: Explain the visual aspects of your design.

D. Sustainability: Explain how your design is sustainable.

Explanation

This is not just a description of the screens. It is your justification for why the wireframe decisions are appropriate for users and the problem.

Functionality

What the design allows users to do and how tasks are completed.

Ergonomics

Comfort, readability, navigation, button placement, and ease of interaction.

Aesthetics

Layout clarity, hierarchy, spacing, consistency, and visual experience.

Sustainability

Long-term use, scalability, maintainability, and reducing unnecessary effort.

User Impact

Explain why each design decision improves the user experience.

Useful Sentence Starters

In my wireframes, users can... / This supports ergonomics because... / The visual layout uses... / The design is sustainable because... / This decision helps users by...
