Assessment 1: Build and maintain a business relationship through a UX partnership strategy.

This guide helps you complete A50206 Understanding the UX Workplace. You are working as a UX Designer at Uber, identifying a real driver experience problem and developing a UX solution supported by a strategic business partner.

Read the assessment text first, then use the explanation and evidence checklist to make sure each response is specific, researched, and connected to the same project idea.

How To Use This Guide

2

Follow the tips

Use the guide to plan your own answer. Keep the same driver problem, UX solution, partner, and objectives connected across every question.

3

Check the evidence

Simple Way To Work Through Each Question

Choose

Choose one real Uber driver problem that you can research and explain clearly.

Connect

Connect every later answer back to that same problem so the project feels like one strategy.

Research

Use Uber pages, reports, recent articles, industry data, or partner websites as evidence.

Explain

Write in your own words and explain why the solution helps drivers and Uber as a business.

Present

Turn the written strategy into a clear slide presentation with a confident recorded delivery.

Student Support

Keep One Clear Project Line

Students often get lost when each answer feels separate. Use this sequence as your project spine from Task 1 to Task 2.

ProblemWhat is difficult, unclear, unsafe, slow, expensive, or stressful for Uber drivers?
SolutionWhat UX feature, service flow, dashboard, support tool, or process would improve the driver experience?
PartnerWhich external organisation can help Uber deliver the solution with real capability?
ObjectivesHow will Uber, the partner, and you measure success?
PresentationHow will you explain the strategy professionally to Uber stakeholders?

Academic Integrity

AI Rules For This Assessment

Assessment Text
AI tools may be used to support your thinking and structuring. However, you must demonstrate your own research, problem identification, UX decisions, and business reasoning. AI must not generate your final responses, solution, or strategy.
Explanation

You can use AI to clarify concepts, organise notes, or improve grammar. You cannot use AI to invent the final Uber problem, final UX solution, final partner justification, SMART goals, or presentation content for you. Your evidence and reasoning must be real and explainable.

Evidence Checklist

Task 1

Establish Business Relationships

Assessment Text
Your task is to identify a real driver experience problem and develop a UX + business partnership strategy to solve it. You will: identify a real UX issue affecting drivers; propose a UX solution; identify and justify a strategic partner; define clear business and networking objectives; present your strategy professionally.
Explanation

Task 1 is one connected project. Do not choose a different idea for every answer. Pick one Uber driver issue, then build a solution, partner decision, and SMART objectives around that same issue.

Evidence Checklist

Task 1 | Q1

Identify a Driver Experience Problem

Assessment Text
Research Uber using: official website; reports, articles, or recent industry insights. Identify one (1) clear problem affecting driver experience. Examples include earnings transparency, safety features, support systems, onboarding or usability issues, and communication between drivers and platform. Explain: What is the problem? Why is it important? How does it impact drivers? How does this issue relate to Uber's focus on safety, reliability, and user experience? Minimum 120 words. Must include at least 1 referenced source.
Explanation

Start with research, not opinion. Choose one driver problem and explain it clearly. A strong answer names the issue, shows why it matters, describes the impact on drivers, and connects the issue to Uber's goals of safety, reliability, and user experience.

Useful structure:

Uber drivers may experience [problem]. This matters because [driver impact]. Evidence from [source] shows [specific detail]. This connects to Uber's focus on [safety/reliability/user experience] because [reason].

Evidence Checklist

Task 1 | Q2

Propose a UX Solution and Partnership Strategy

Assessment Text
You must design a practical UX solution that addresses the problem you identified in Q1, and explain how this solution would be implemented through a partnership with another organisation. Explain your UX solution, the role of the strategic partner, and the impact on driver experience. Minimum 120 words. Must clearly connect: problem -> solution -> partner -> outcome. Your solution must be realistic and implementable within Uber's platform.
Explanation

This answer is where your idea becomes practical. Name your UX solution, describe how it works, then explain why Uber would need a partner. The partner must contribute something specific, such as data, technology, safety expertise, training, payments support, or service delivery.

ProblemWhat driver issue did Q1 identify?
SolutionWhat feature, process, tool, or service would reduce the problem?
PartnerWho helps Uber implement it?
OutcomeWhat changes for drivers and for Uber?

Evidence Checklist

Task 1 | Q3

Select and Justify a Strategic Partner

Assessment Text
Identify at least two (2) organisations that could support your solution. For each organisation, explain what the organisation does, why they are relevant to your solution, and what they could contribute. From your two options, select one (1) organisation as your final strategic partner. You must compare both options before selecting the best partner. Explain why this partner is the best fit, what specific value they bring to Uber, how they improve the driver experience, and evidence to support your decision. Minimum 150 words.
Explanation

Do not jump straight to one partner. Compare two real organisations first. Then choose the strongest option and justify why it fits your solution better. Your comparison should be specific: capability, relevance, cost, trust, data, technology, driver benefit, and business value.

Comparison sentence starter:

Partner A is useful because [capability], while Partner B is useful because [capability]. However, Partner [A/B] is the better choice for this project because [reason linked to the driver problem and UX solution].

Evidence Checklist

Task 1 | Q4

Define Business and Networking Objectives

Assessment Text
You must create three (3) SMART objectives for your project: 1. Business Development Objective (For Uber), 2. Networking Objective (Uber + Partner), and 3. Professional Development Objective (You as UX Designer). Each objective must clearly include what will be achieved, how it will be measured, and when it will be achieved.
Explanation

A SMART objective is not a vague wish. It needs a clear action, a measurable result, and a deadline. Write one objective for Uber's business result, one for the partnership relationship, and one for your own development as the UX designer.

BusinessWhat should improve for Uber, such as satisfaction, retention, efficiency, or trust?
NetworkingHow will Uber and the partner build, maintain, and evaluate the relationship?
ProfessionalWhat UX skill will you improve during the project?

Evidence Checklist

Task 2

Maintain Business Relationships

Assessment Text
Your proposal has been reviewed internally, and you have now been invited to present your idea to Uber stakeholders. This is your opportunity to clearly communicate your UX solution, demonstrate the value of your strategic partnership, and convince decision-makers that your idea is worth implementing.
Explanation

Task 2 converts your written strategy into a professional presentation. Your slides and video should tell the same story as Task 1: the driver problem, your solution, the partner, the objectives, the value, the risks, and how the relationship will continue.

Evidence Checklist

Task 2 | Q1

Professional Presentation

Assessment Text
You must prepare and deliver a professional presentation that communicates your full project. Your presentation must cover: introduction, the problem, your UX solution, strategic partner, strategy overview, objectives, value of your solution, risks and barriers, long-term relationship, promotion strategy, and questions and discussion. You must deliver a recorded presentation (8-12 minutes) and show your screen and yourself presenting. You must submit your presentation file and your video recording.
Explanation

Use a simple presentation structure. One idea per slide is enough. Your goal is to sound like a UX designer explaining a realistic business strategy to Uber stakeholders, not reading long paragraphs from the screen.

StartTitle, purpose, problem, and why it matters.
MiddleSolution, partner, implementation, objectives, and value.
FinishRisks, long-term relationship, promotion strategy, questions, and conclusion.
